SEO

Search Engine Optimization

Search engine optimization (SEO) is a strategy that enhances the quality and quantity of website traffic from search engines, focusing on unpaid traffic from various search types like image, video, academic, news, and industry-specific vertical search engines. SEO considers search engine behavior, user search, search terms, and preferred search engines. It is performed to increase website traffic and convert visitors into customers, as higher rankings on the search engine results page lead to more visitors.

GI

"Getting indexed "

Google, Bing, and Yahoo! use crawlers to find pages for their algorithmic search results. Pages linked from other search engine-indexed pages are automatically found, while the Yahoo! Directory and DMOZ require manual submission and human editorial review. Google offers the Google Search Console, which allows for the creation and submission of an XML Sitemap feed for free. Yahoo! previously operated a paid submission service, which guaranteed crawling for a cost per click, but discontinued in 2009. Search engine crawlers consider various factors, including the distance of pages from the root directory. In 2016, Google made their index mobile-first, starting with the mobile version of a website. In 2019, Google updated their crawler's rendering engine to the latest version of Chromium and began updating the User-Agent string to reflect the latest Chrome version used by their rendering service.

WH Vs BH

"White hat versus black hat techniques"

SEO techniques can be classified into two categories: white hat SEO, which is recommended by search engine companies, and black hat SEO, which is not approved by search engines. White hat SEO ensures that content indexed by search engines is the same as user-generated content, making it easily accessible to search engines. Black hat SEO aims to improve rankings in ways disapproved of by search engines or involve deception, such as using hidden text or cloaking. Grey hat SEO is a middle ground, focusing on improving search engine rankings without producing the best content for users. Search engines may penalize sites using black or grey hat methods, either by reducing rankings or eliminating listings from their databases. However, both companies apologized, fixed offending pages, and were restored to Google's search engine results page.

Marking Stratery

SEO is not an appropriate strategy for every website, and other Internet marketing strategies can be more effective, such as paid advertising through pay-per-click (PPC) campaigns, depending on the site operator's goals.

A successful SEO campaign may include high-quality web pages, analytics programs, and improving conversion rates. Google has shifted its focus towards "usefulness" and mobile local search, encouraging websites to use the Mobile-Friendly Test to measure their website's user-friendliness. While SEO may generate a return on investment, search engines are not paid for organic search traffic and their algorithms can change, impacting a website's ranking and potentially causing significant loss of traffic. Website operators should avoid relying heavily on search engine traffic and focus on user web accessibility.
